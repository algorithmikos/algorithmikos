I came to software through language. A background in German Studies taught me that every system — whether a grammar, a culture, or a codebase — has an underlying architecture worth understanding before you touch it. That instinct shapes how I write software: deliberately, with care for structure, and with respect for the people who will maintain it after me.

I write in **TypeScript** because ambiguity is expensive. I build with **Node.js and React** because I know them well enough to be honest about their limits. And I am currently learning **Python and NLP** — because meaning encoded in text is a problem I find genuinely worth solving.

## What I'm Building

**Nizamiyyah** · *Live*
An LMS platform for teachers, students, and guardians. Multi-role, session-aware, and built with care. Each role sees exactly what it needs: teachers log sessions and grade recitations, students track their own progress, guardians follow along with full transparency. This is the work I am most proud of.

**Bethink** · *In Development*
A language-agnostic CLI release orchestrator that automates the overhead of software versioning. It detects manifest files across Node.js, Python, PHP, and Go — then uses a local LLM to turn raw commit history into professional release documentation. Three actions: bump the version, commit with AI-assisted messaging, tag the release. Built for developers who care about documentation hygiene but refuse to be slowed down by it.

**MathMind** · *In Development*
An AI-integrated mathematics learning application for school students. The goal is not to replace a teacher — it is to give every student the patience of one.

*Earlier projects — Teryaq (a Type 1 diabetes companion app) and Conquer (personal task management) — remain functional and are part of the journey that brought me here.*
